About

Revamping Beam's Vehicle🛴 and User🚴 Management Tool

Role

Sole designer

Period

Jun - Jul 2021

Challenge

1 No PRD or success metrics to guide the design output.

2 Limited time and development resources which required me to identify high-impact issues while planning for future enhancements.

Impact

1 Reduction in time spent by Ops on low agency tasks by reducing unnecessary steps and context-switching.

2 Prioritised and redesigned core screens and flows within a month for a growing ops team.

Previews

💸

Refunds

I redesigned the refunds flow to allow Ops to refund trips as Beam credits or to riders' original payment method. While an open field would have been acceptable, I felt that more could be done to make it more efficient, eventually ending up with a design that's optimised for the most common Ops use-cases.

🕵️

User Lookup

As the Ops team often switched between multiple platforms to gather supporting info about a Rider in order to decide if they should be refunded, I redesigned the User Lookup to surface their trip and transaction history from Mixpanel and Redash to reduce the number of platforms Ops had to access for their day-to-day operations.

🛴

Vehicle Management

As we were planning to scale the Operations team and expand the capability of the Ops tool, the information architecture was a key consideration in the redesign. I was able to take on a product role helping to prioritise and scope requirements, future-proof pages and features, and organising Ops requests and feedback.

📍

Map Filters and Markers

I approached the redesign by consolidating the key tasks-to-be-done, resulting in the legend-centric approach as it was the Ops priority to keep inoperable vehicles off the road, and keep the operating vehicles well-distributed. I also wanted to avoid overwhelming Ops with overly granular controls, so I had to pinpoint the filters our ops team were actually using to decide the level of control that was necessary to display as we did not track usage previously.
