About

Optimising the refund flow to improve Ops efficiency

Role

Senior Product Designer @ Beam

Status

Launched Aug 2021

Challenge

Identifying the problem, defining the goals, designing the solution, and testing with internal users all within a limited timeframe.

Impact

Reduced time spent initiating refunds which enables the Ops team to focus on other priorities. Also set up the foundations for the Internal Ops Tool.

01

Background

Why refunds?

I was tasked to redesign the Beam internal tool. I discovered that the operations team navigated between 3 to 4 platforms each time when making refunds to riders.

02

Problems & opportunities

No easy access to customer history

The Ops team mentioned that they would refer to supporting information like trip and credit history when deciding whether a refund is warranted.

Currently, this info is only accessible through Redash. As only Ops is authorised to access this data, this makes it difficult to outsource refunds to Support staff.

Can't easily refund to card

Most refunds are issued as credits because it's more cost-effective. The Ops team typically offers bonus credits to avoid making card refunds, but some customers prefer refunds to their original payment method, which requires Ops to process the refund through Adyen.

Additionally...

To speed up development, I also selected and adapted Ant’s comprehensive design system instead of trying to work with the existing component library which was restrictive and had no Figma equivalent. To help familiarise Engineers with the new UI library, the work was prioritised in stages starting with the Refund modal.

03

Explorations

Early attempts

From desk research, I noted that Adyen and other refund modals typically featured an open entry field. The Ops team also requested that I keep the ability to issue custom credits to riders, though these amounts vary so I did not make them a shortcut.

So I kept the flexibility of an open field and made credit refunds the default action as credits were more cost-effective and encouraged user retention.

However, I felt more could be done. I continued exploring options focused on reducing the number of inputs with the goal of making refunds more efficient, thus reducing the amount of time Ops spends on routine work.

Back to the drawing board

After a round of feedback from the Ops team, I pivoted to designing around the most-common use cases with the goal of making refunds more efficient. I wanted the Ops team to be able to make refunds with just one click.

In the final solution, Ops could toggle on and off items they wished to refund without having to manually enter the amount which also helps reduce mistypes. If Ops needed to refund bonus credits to customers (eg. KRW600 on a KRW500 total), they could still do so via the custom credits field.

04

Result & impact

Impact #1

By integrating card refunds into the internal Ops tool, less time is spent navigating to a different platform, searching up a transaction, and entering the refund amount. This translates to extra time for the Ops team to focus on other priorities.

Impact #2

I also set up the design foundations for the new Internal Ops tool, using pre-made components and data visualisation libraries as time-to-launch was a key measure of success.

This enabled engineers to focus on building the internal Ops tool instead of getting lost in the weeds.
